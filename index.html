Grant J. Snitker

PHD Candidate, Arizona State University


I am a Ph.D. candidate in archaeology at Arizona State University. I am an environmental archaeologist with primary interests in long-term dynamics of human socio-ecological systems. My research interests center on understanding the feedbacks between human societies and earth-systems during periods of major transformation, with particular attention paid to the spread of agro-pastoral subsistence strategies. I approach these issues through an active research program of fieldwork and laboratory analyses to examine the social and ecological drivers of anthropogenic fire and its long-term impact on landscape dynamics. I employ a wide range of archaeological sampling and analytical strategies, as well as methods from paleoecology, fire ecology, and computational modeling in ecologically and culturally similar study regions in Spain and the U.S. Southwest. My work concentrates on integrating archaeology into broader, interdisciplinary efforts to understand the origins of the anthropogenic landscapes.

Equal to my research interests is my passion for communicating science to the broader public through museum-based educational programs and exhibit development. For over a decade, I have bridged the gap between collaborative archaeological research and museological theory and practices. I also specialize in archaeological collections research and management.

Outside of academics, I spend my free time hiking, camping, and fly-fishing.
